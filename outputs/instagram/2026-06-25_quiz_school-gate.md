## THE QUIZ (overlay text)
- Scenario line: "You pick up Sam. The meester smiles and says: 'Hij heeft lekker gespeeld vandaag.'"
- Question: "Wat bedoelt de meester?" (What does the teacher mean?)
- Options:
  A) He played happily today.
  B) He ate something tasty today.
  C) He has to play more.
- Correct: **A** — here "lekker" has nothing to do with food. With an activity it means it went nicely, comfortably, with ease.

## REVEAL (slide 2 or pinned comment)
It's A. "Lekker" isn't only about taste. Paired with an activity it means it went well: "lekker gespeeld" = had a good play, "lekker geslapen" = slept well, "lekker gewerkt" = got a good stretch of work done. So the meester is telling you Sam had a nice day. A warm reply back: "Wat fijn, dank je wel!" (How nice, thank you!).
