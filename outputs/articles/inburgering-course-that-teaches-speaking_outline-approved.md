## Section 5 — What a production-based course feels like to use
**H2:** `What a production-based course feels like to use`
**Word target:** 350

**Key argument:** Show, don't label. Walk the lesson loop; the lesson is done when you've used the new vocabulary yourself. Feature → benefit → proof for each point.

**Open with a bolded one-line TL;DR.**

**Assigned proof points (mechanism + early-tester voice; describe, don't name the method):**
- The two-phase loop: practice phase (each small unit read-and-speak, listen-and-speak, then write, all three before moving on; practice mixes in review) → conversation phase (Joost stays in the scenario, makes a new conversation, prompts you to use the lesson's words, responds to what you actually say — not a script). *(product-design-evidence.md §2.)* No jargon.
- **Level-matched replies** — Feature: Joost only uses words you've practiced. Benefit: usable, not overwhelming. Proof: "He's not gonna use C2 level Dutch to reply to you."
- **Words in context** — Feature: vocabulary used in conversation, not seen on a card. Benefit: transfers to real talking. Proof: "The words that you learn you use… it helps me communicate."
- **Guided, low cognitive load** — Feature: the lesson is structured for you. Benefit: energy goes to Dutch, not planning. Proof: "I don't have the cognitive load of trying to engineer my lesson… it's just guided."

**EVIDENCE GAP — do not fill:** no quantified retention / pass-and-speak metric, no case study exists. Carry the section on mechanism + voice only. Optionally state plainly that the course is in early testing (honest credibility move). Do NOT invent a number.

## Section 6 — Choosing the right fit for your situation
**H2:** `Choosing the right fit for your situation`
**Word target:** 250

**Key argument:** Close goals-first, not product-first. Let the reader decide. Soft CTA only, no price.

**Assigned proof points (honest fit guidance — competitive-landscape "How We Win" / "Where We Lose"):**
- Pick a **classroom** if you need peer accountability and a human teacher relationship.
- Pick a **tutor** if you want one-to-one human conversation and budget isn't the constraint.
- Pick a **cheap memorization course** if you only need the certificate fast and don't care about retention — and note honestly that for people who hate memorizing, learning by using can be the faster route (don't assume memorization is faster for everyone).
- Pick a **production-based self-paced course** if you're self-motivated, want to speak on your own schedule, and want to finish able to talk.
- Honest fit limit: already comfortable at A2 and need B1 now? An A1–A2 course isn't your next step.
