## Opening definition paragraph (AEO — featured snippet target)

**Target query:** "Why can't I speak Dutch after passing the inburgering exam?"
Lead the article with a clean, self-contained answer before any narrative. Use roughly this substance (rewrite in voice, do not copy verbatim):

> Many people pass the inburgering exam and still can't speak Dutch because the exam and most courses reward recognition and memorized responses rather than spontaneous conversation, and A2 is a low enough bar that holding a real conversation isn't required to pass. It isn't a personal failure; it's how the system is built. The upside: passing means you already recognize a lot of Dutch, so the remaining task is converting that passive knowledge into speech through regular, level-matched conversation practice.
