## Section 2 — Who Dutchwithjoost is NOT for
- **H2:** Who Dutchwithjoost is NOT for
- **Word target:** 480
- **Key argument:** Five honest disqualifiers, each with a plain reason and, where possible, a better alternative for that person.
- **Structure (use H3s or bolded leads per disqualifier):**
  1. **You're on a municipality-assigned or government-funded integration track.** When the municipality assigns and funds your course, you usually have to pick from their approved provider list. Check your track before buying anything — including this. *(Frame as "check your track first." Do NOT say "we're not on DUO's approved list.")*
  2. **You can already hold basic conversations at an A2 level and need B1 today.** Then this isn't your next step. The course takes a beginner up to A2; a dedicated B1 course is coming, but if you need B1 right now, get a B1 option for the time being. **Nuance, write it softly:** a beginner who will *eventually* need B1 is still a fit, because everyone passes through A1 and A2 first.
  3. **You want classroom accountability** — a teacher in the room and classmates expecting you. That's a real need we don't serve. Go classroom. *(Proof: anonymized parent learner on a government-funded track — "I personally benefit from an in-person class. The interaction with other students... I'm a bit more driven to learn that way." Attribute as "one parent we spoke to," no name.)*
  4. **Your budget is genuinely zero.** Free and very cheap resources exist; use them, no spin.
  5. **You only want to pass as fast as possible** and don't care about speaking afterward. **Honest, careful framing:** if you're someone who learns well by memorizing, a memorization-based course might get you to a pass quicker — but that's a real "if." Plenty of people (our own co-founder included) can't make memorization stick, and for them the "fast" route isn't fast at all. We're built around actually using the language, which asks more of you up front. Don't claim memorization is faster for everyone.
- **Proof points:** anonymized classroom learner (Evidence A); honest budget/free-resource acknowledgment; conditional (not universal) speed trade-off on memorization.
