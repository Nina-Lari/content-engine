## Thread 8 — The work / family / self-care / learning balance (mom post)

**Post:**
> "Struggles with Dutch learning. Hi everyone, I'm curious how you balance work and Dutch learning. I have a full-time mentally challenging job. We have a kid, so after work (around 18:00) we try to spend time together (our kid goes to bed around 19:20). I exercise regularly because of my back problems (2x per week after our kid goes to sleep and 1x on the weekend). I am trying to learn Dutch. But as you can imagine, it is quite difficult to have energy and motivation to learn after a full working day. What are your experiences? How do you manage to balance work-family-self-care-learning Dutch? We don't have any family here, so it's only my partner, me, and our kid."

**Comments:**
> "I book meetings in my calendar on my wfh days and do it then."

> "Uggghhh… I know the feeling! I actually woke up every day at 5am just to be able to learn Dutch for my inburgering. I tried to always go to bed between 21:00-22:00 so I could wake up that early just to study because both my little kids always woke up at 06:30 like clockwork! I am sooooooo glad and relieved that that stage of my life is done and over with! Ik ben eindelijk een Nederlander."

> "Yup it's super hard. I find it hard to find the time, esp when the goal is to learn for myself (no exams etc), I feel my motivation also wavers."

> "Sounds like me, I do 'lunch break' Dutch lesson on a workday. Very slow progress, but more than zero."

> "Same, small child, no family and i work 40h. Luckily i wfh 2-3 days and those are the days for cleaning, washing clothes… household tasks. Because we try on weekends to do family things… My child is going 5 days to daycare. I decided to not take the exams and will take another 5 years residency permit. I do try to keep up my dutch on Duolingo and Busuu. And read some basic grammar books. But for me is impossible. Both me and my husband are working 40h and we don't have me/self care time… We work in IT so we work with English language. ps I breastfeed my son until 2 years and he never sleep whole night so i also don't have longer sleep then 4h so my concentration is very low."

> "Before my freelance job, I usually try to study for 1 hr. My husband takes care of the kids in the evening while I study. Right now my 1 hour 'free time' is occupied with fee proposals and tender submissions. The only time I get to 'study' dutch is when my taalmatje and I meet up for an hour. It is truly difficult if you don't have a support system. It is slow and it takes time. I'm always tired."

> "Mom of 4 and living in the Netherlands for 7 years. I also work full time, manage a business, and a women's community… My job is quite intense as I manage approx 20 direct reports. Luckily, English is our business language. I feel embarrassed when people talk to me in Dutch and I can't manage to spontaneously reply without loading like a slow computer. it is especially embarrassing because I have a Dutch passport yet I just do not have the time nor the drive to learn at a higher level… The extra time I do have, I would prefer to spend it with my family or friends. As it is, I have to choose between keeping a clean house or bonding with my kids. I always choose the latter, and just pay someone to do the cleaning. It is really tough."

> "In my office there are some people who volunteer to have lunch in Dutch with those who need to practice. Doable during work hours and quality time with colleagues."

> "I feel you… I usually try to watch any dutch Drama in NPO that helps a lot to learn the speaking and vocabulary. I recommend Taal Tempo to make you fluent in speaking and also answering automatically. I find that this book really shapes my reflex to speak dutch. Beside that maybe 'Klaar met de taal' book is my recommendation for support to learn grammatica."

> "Turn on Dutch radio, for example BNR, or any audiobook, podcast. Than just repeat everything they said. This can help you."

> "The best way is to incorporate learning throughout your daily life! If you are listening to music during workouts, turn on Dutch music. Podcasts? Dutch learning podcasts or even listen to the news in Dutch. If you're able to sneak in some Netflix before you go to bed? Throw on those Dutch subtitles. it's all about being able to integrate it into your current day life, to be honest (and maybe a little blunt), no one ever 'has time' to learn a language. But if you take these little micro steps daily, you'll start to see small differences that will add up over time."

> "Does your child talk yet? Maybe you could practice simple Dutch words or rhymes together… Watch cartoons in Dutch together, listen to children's songs in Dutch… and read children's books… This way you won't lose the time you spend with your child, and at the same time you'll be learning."

> "Hey! I have a similar routine as yours and I have 2 small kids. I am attending group lessons once a week (online) from 19:15-21:30 and I do half an hour private lessons during my lunch time, also once a week. On top of that, I listen to dutch radio, dutch podcasts while I work out, I am reading a dutch teenager romance and I try to speak as much as I can. This has helped me a lot to improve my language proficiency."

> "I don't have such a busy schedule but at the end of the day also feel like there's too much on my plate. I use the nos journal in makkelijke taal and the app Topic Today. They're both short and can support the language learning process for busy people."

> "Hi there! What works for me is having a guided class — with tests but without set schedule. I'm taking an online class that is self study but with assignments (writing, speaking and a few meet ups to practice). I study early morning at 6:00, I'm on my desk before the kids wake up — and I meet my classmate to practice at 20:00 after they go to sleep once a week only. You got this!"

> "I took several courses, each lasting about 2 months. During that time, I dedicated all my free time to learning Dutch, studying on trains, at airports, and everywhere. My husband was super supportive, taking care of everything else."
