# Competitor Stance Addendum

> Detailed guidelines for writing about specific competitors. Use this alongside the messaging document and factual accuracy checklist.

**Principle:** Describe what each approach does. Acknowledge their strengths. Let readers decide which fits their goals. Never talk down on competitors or imply their approach is lazy, easy, or inferior.

**Last updated:** 2026-05-28

---

## InburgeringOnline

The market leader for online inburgering preparation. They are our primary comparison point.

### Verified Facts

| Fact              | Value                                         | Verification needed?                   |
| ----------------- | --------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| Pricing           | €24.95 - €34.95/month (subscription)          | Check their website before publishing  |
| Trustpilot rating | ~4.4 stars                                    | Check current rating before publishing |
| Method            | Video lessons, practice exercises, mock exams | Verified from website                  |
| Coverage          | All exam components                           | Verified                               |
| Endorsed by       | DutchReview recommends them                   | Verified                               |

### What They Do Well

Acknowledge these strengths when relevant:

- **Established track record:** Many people have passed using their course. This is documented in reviews.
- **Comprehensive coverage:** They cover all exam components including KNM.
- **Structured path:** Users know what to do next.
- **Mock exams:** Help learners understand the exam format.
- **Lower monthly commitment:** Subscription model lets people try before committing to a large sum.

### Their Honest Limitations

These are documented in user feedback (Trustpilot, Reddit), not our opinion:

- **Recognition vs. production:** Their approach focuses on recognizing correct answers. The speaking component is less emphasized than reading/listening.
- **Pass but can't speak:** This complaint pattern appears in community discussions from learners who used memorization-based courses.

### How to Write About Them

**In comparison content (BOFU):**

- Name them directly: "InburgeringOnline"
- State facts: subscription pricing, video lessons, flash cards, mock exams
- Explain the difference neutrally: "InburgeringOnline focuses on exam preparation through video lessons, flash cards, and practice tests. Dutchwithjoost focuses on conversation practice, with the exam as a natural result."

**In educational content (MOFU/TOFU):**

- Reference them as "memorization-based courses" or "online courses that focus on exam preparation"
- Do not name them unless you're making a specific factual point

### Language Guide

| Do NOT say                            | Say instead                                  |
| ------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| "Just memorization"                   | "Memorization-based approach"                |
| "Clicking through screens"            | "Video lessons and practice exercises"       |
| "Easy to build"                       | Do not mention                               |
| "They focus on quantity over quality" | Do not say (judgmental)                      |
| "Cheat sheet to the exam"             | Do not say (too harsh)                       |
| "They have more track record than us" | Do not mention track record comparison       |
| "We're newer"                         | Do not mention                               |
| "Their approach is faster"            | Do not compare speed                         |
| "They only teach you to pass"         | "Their approach focuses on exam preparation" |

### When to Mention Them

| Content type                       | Mention by name? | How to frame                                     |
| ---------------------------------- | ---------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| Comparison article                 | Yes              | Neutral side-by-side                             |
| "Best inburgering courses" roundup | Yes              | As one option among several                      |
| "Why speaking practice matters"    | No               | Reference "memorization-based courses" generally |
| Thought leadership                 | No               | Do not mention competitors                       |

## Duolingo

Common question: "Can I use Duolingo for inburgering?"

### Verified Facts

| Fact                         | Value                                |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| Price                        | Free (with ads) or Plus subscription |
| Inburgering-specific content | No                                   |
| KNM coverage                 | No                                   |
| Speaking practice            | Minimal (repeat-after-me style)      |
| Gamification                 | Strong (streaks, XP, leaderboards)   |

### What They Do Well

- **Habit formation:** The gamification keeps people coming back daily.
- **Low barrier to entry:** Free, no commitment required.
- **Basics:** Good for absolute beginners getting their first exposure to Dutch.
- **Vocabulary building:** Teaches common words through repetition.

### Their Honest Limitations

These are documented in community discussions:

- **Not designed for inburgering:** Does not cover KNM, does not match exam format.
- **Limited speaking practice:** Speaking exercises are mostly repeat-after-me, not conversation.
- **Impractical phrases:** Community memes about learning phrases like "the horse drinks beer" that never appear in real life.
- **Recognition over production:** Multiple choice format trains recognition, not production.

### How to Write About Them

**When someone asks "Can I use Duolingo?":**

Fair answer: "Duolingo is useful for building a daily habit and learning basic vocabulary. It's not designed for the inburgering exam, so it won't prepare you for the KNM section or the exam format. Many people use it alongside other preparation."

**Do NOT say:**

- "Duolingo is useless"
- "Duolingo won't help you"
- "Duolingo teaches you nothing"

**DO say:**

- "Duolingo isn't designed for exam preparation"
- "Duolingo focuses on vocabulary, not conversation"
- "Duolingo can supplement other learning but won't cover everything you need"

### Language Guide

| Do NOT say                    | Say instead                                      |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| "Useless for inburgering"     | "Not designed for inburgering"                   |
| "Just a game"                 | "Gamified learning app"                          |
| "You'll learn nothing useful" | "Focuses on vocabulary basics"                   |
| "Waste of time"               | "Good for habit building, limited for exam prep" |

## Classroom Courses

Multiple providers: Koentact, TaalBoost, Direct Dutch, university language centers, community programs.

### Verified Facts

| Fact          | Typical range              |
| ------------- | -------------------------- |
| Price         | €500 - €2,000 for A2 level |
| Format        | Scheduled group sessions   |
| Class size    | 8-15 students typically    |
| Duration      | 3-6 months typically       |
| Human teacher | Yes                        |

### What They Do Well

- **Human accountability:** Teacher tracks progress, corrects in person.
- **Social environment:** Learning with peers, making friends with other expats.
- **Structured schedule:** External accountability helps some learners.
- **Cultural context:** Teachers explain nuances that self-study misses.
- **Immediate feedback:** Teacher can clarify confusion on the spot.

### Their Honest Limitations

These are trade-offs, not criticisms:

- **Fixed schedules:** You must attend at specific times. Hard if you work irregular hours or have family obligations.
- **Speaking time divided:** With 10 students and one teacher, individual speaking practice is limited.
- **Price:** €500-2,000 is a significant investment.
- **Pace mismatch:** The class moves at an average pace. You might be faster or slower than the group.
- **Location dependency:** You need to be in the right city or travel.

### How to Write About Them

**Fair comparison:**
"Classroom courses provide a social environment and human teacher. Speaking time is divided among students. Fixed schedules work well for people who want external accountability. Self-paced options work better for people with irregular schedules or who want more individual practice time."

**When recommending:**
"Classroom courses are a good choice if you value social learning, want a human teacher, and can commit to a fixed schedule."

### Language Guide

| Do NOT say                                | Say instead                               |
| ----------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| "Expensive"                               | State the price range, let readers judge  |
| "Rigid schedules"                         | "Fixed schedules"                         |
| "You only get 5 minutes of speaking time" | "Speaking time is divided among students" |
| "Outdated"                                | Do not say                                |
| "You're just a number"                    | Do not say                                |
| "Teachers aren't qualified"               | Do not say (often untrue)                 |

## Generic AI Tools (ChatGPT, etc.)

People ask: "Can I just use ChatGPT to practice Dutch?"

### Verified Facts

| Fact                  | Value                             |
| --------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| Price                 | Free or subscription              |
| Curriculum            | None (open-ended)                 |
| Exam-specific content | None                              |
| Conversation ability  | Yes (unlimited)                   |
| Dutch accuracy        | Generally good, occasional errors |

### What They Do Well

- **Unlimited conversation:** You can practice as much as you want.
- **No judgment:** You can make mistakes without embarrassment.
- **Available 24/7:** Practice anytime.
- **Free or low cost:** No financial barrier.
- **Flexible topics:** You can discuss whatever you want.

### Their Honest Limitations

- **No curriculum:** You have to decide what to practice. No guidance on what you need for the exam.
- **No KNM:** Won't teach you Dutch society knowledge.
- **No exam format:** Won't prepare you for the specific exam structure.
- **No progression tracking:** You don't know if you're improving or ready for the exam.
- **No inline grammar feedback:** If you make a grammar mistake, ChatGPT either ignores it or stops the conversation to correct you. There's no way to get feedback without breaking the flow.
- **No pronunciation feedback:** ChatGPT is text-based. It cannot hear you speak or tell you if your pronunciation is correct.
- **Quality varies:** Depends on how you prompt it.

### How to Write About Them

**Fair framing:**
"Generic AI tools provide open-ended conversation practice. They don't follow a curriculum or cover what you need for the exam. Some people use them to supplement structured learning."

**Do NOT say:**

- "AI can't teach you Dutch" (people have passed using AI)
- "ChatGPT is random" (use "unstructured")
- "You need a real course, not AI" (dismissive)

**Critical warning:** Do NOT include quotes like "I passed all exams with AI alone" in content. This undermines our positioning.

### Language Guide

| Do NOT say                | Say instead                                            |
| ------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| "Random practice"         | "Unstructured conversation practice"                   |
| "AI doesn't work"         | "AI doesn't follow a curriculum"                       |
| "You can't learn from AI" | "AI practice works best alongside structured learning" |
| "Chatbots are basic"      | "General-purpose AI isn't designed for exam prep"      |

## Free Resources (YouTube, Websites, etc.)

### What Exists

- **YouTube channels:** Dutch Pronunciation, Learn Dutch with Niels, DutchPod101
- **Free websites:** Duolingo, netinnederland.nl (government), oefenen.nl
- **DUO practice exams:** Free official practice exams on inburgeren.nl
- **Language exchange apps:** Tandem, HelloTalk, ConversationExchange

### What They Do Well

- **No cost:** Accessible to everyone.
- **Variety:** Different learning styles covered.
- **Community:** Language exchange connects you with real people.
- **Official practice:** DUO practice exams show you the real format.

### Their Honest Limitations

- **No structure:** You have to piece together your own curriculum.
- **Quality varies:** Some YouTube content is excellent, some is poor.
- **Self-discipline required:** No accountability or progress tracking.
- **Speaking practice limited:** Hard to get consistent conversation practice.

### How to Write About Them

**Fair framing:**
"Free resources can supplement structured learning. DUO provides official practice exams that show you the exam format. YouTube has pronunciation guides. Language exchange apps connect you with speaking partners. The challenge is creating your own structure."

**When to recommend:**

- Always mention DUO practice exams (they're official and free)
- Recommend YouTube for pronunciation
- Mention language exchange for conversation practice (while noting scheduling challenges)

## Babbel, Pimsleur, Rosetta Stone

Traditional language learning apps. Occasionally mentioned in inburgering contexts.

### What They Do

- **Babbel:** Structured lessons, grammar focus, some conversation practice
- **Pimsleur:** Audio-based, listening and repetition
- **Rosetta Stone:** Immersive approach, no English explanations

### How to Write About Them

**Fair framing:**
"Apps like Babbel and Pimsleur teach general Dutch, not specifically for inburgering. They're useful for overall language skills but won't cover KNM or the exam format."

**Do NOT position them as direct competitors.** They serve a different purpose (general Dutch learning vs. exam preparation).

## Taalhammer and Flashcard Apps (Anki, etc.)

### What They Do

- **Taalhammer:** Sentence-based vocabulary learning with spaced repetition
- **Anki:** Customizable flashcard system with spaced repetition
- **Quizlet:** Shared flashcard decks, including inburgering-specific ones

### What They Do Well

- Spaced repetition helps with retention
- Taalhammer specifically addresses "words in isolation" problem
- Anki is highly customizable

### How to Write About Them

**Fair framing:**
"Flashcard apps help with vocabulary retention. Taalhammer specifically addresses the problem of learning words in isolation by teaching sentences. These tools work well for memorization but don't provide conversation practice."

**They support our positioning:** Taalhammer's own marketing says "isolated words fade away" which validates our conversation-based approach.

## Comparison Content Guidelines

### Structure for Fair Comparison Articles

1. **Lead with the reader's goal:** "You want to pass the inburgering exam. Here are your options."

2. **Explain each option neutrally:**
   - What it does
   - What it costs
   - What it's best for
   - Limitations (as trade-offs, not criticisms)

3. **State the difference:**
   - "InburgeringOnline focuses on exam preparation through video lessons and mock exams."
   - "Dutchwithjoost focuses on conversation practice, with the exam as a natural result."

4. **Let the reader decide:** "If you want X, consider Y. If you want Z, consider W."

5. **Include our offer without hard selling:** Brief mention of Exam Pass Guarantee and pricing is OK in BOFU content.

### Prohibited Framing Patterns

| Pattern                          | Why it's prohibited          | Alternative                                       |
| -------------------------------- | ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| "Unlike competitors who only..." | Dismissive, implies inferior | "Our approach focuses on..."                      |
| "The problem with X is..."       | Sets up straw man            | "X is designed for Y. We're designed for Z."      |
| "We're the only course that..."  | Often false, sounds arrogant | "Our approach uses..."                            |
| "If you want real results..."    | Implies others are fake      | "If conversation practice is important to you..." |
| "Stop wasting time with..."      | Dismissive                   | Do not say                                        |
| "You'll actually learn with us"  | Implies others don't work    | "You'll learn through conversation"               |

### Comparison Language That Works

| Instead of                   | Use                                             |
| ---------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| "Better than"                | "Different from" or "focuses on X instead of Y" |
| "Unlike inferior approaches" | "Our approach"                                  |
| "The right choice"           | "A good fit if you want X"                      |
| "They can't"                 | "They focus on Y"                               |
| "We're the best"             | "We're designed for people who want X"          |

## When to Name Competitors

### Name Directly

- Dedicated comparison articles ("Dutchwithjoost vs. InburgeringOnline")
- "Best inburgering courses" roundup content
- Direct answer to "How does X compare to Y?" questions
- When citing a specific fact about them (with verification)

### Reference Generally

- Educational articles about learning methods
- Thought leadership about speaking practice
- TOFU content where naming competitors feels salesy

**General references:**

- "Memorization-based courses"
- "Subscription-based courses"
- "Classroom courses"
- "Free apps"
- "General language learning apps"
- "AI tools"

### Never Mention

- In thought leadership about Dutch learning in general
- When you don't have verified facts
- When the mention would feel gratuitous or salesy

## Handling Specific Competitor Questions

### "Is InburgeringOnline good?"

**Fair answer:** "InburgeringOnline works well for exam preparation. They have good reviews and many people have passed using their course. Their approach focuses on memorization and mock exams. If speaking practice is a priority for you, that's where Dutchwithjoost differs."

### "Should I use Duolingo for inburgering?"

**Fair answer:** "Duolingo isn't designed for the inburgering exam. It won't cover KNM and doesn't match the exam format. It can help build a daily habit and learn basic vocabulary. Many people use it alongside other preparation."

### "Are classroom courses worth the money?"

**Fair answer:** "Classroom courses are worth it if you value a social environment, human teacher, and fixed schedule. They typically cost €500-2,000 for A2. Speaking time is divided among students. If you want more individual practice time and flexible scheduling, self-paced options may fit better."

### "Can I just use ChatGPT?"

**Fair answer:** "You can practice conversation with ChatGPT. It won't teach you what you need for the KNM section or follow a curriculum. Some people use AI alongside structured learning. If you want guidance on what to learn and when, a course with a curriculum helps."

## Red Lines (Never Cross)

1. **Never claim competitors don't work.** People pass with every approach.

2. **Never mock competitor methods.** "Clicking through screens" and "just memorization" are dismissive.

3. **Never compare track record.** We're newer. Don't mention it.

4. **Never compare speed.** "Faster" or "slower" claims are unverifiable.

5. **Never assume competitor intentions.** We don't know why they built their course the way they did.

6. **Never use user complaints as attacks.** Trustpilot complaints are about billing issues, not learning quality. Using them as ammunition is unfair.

7. **Never say "we're the only course that..."** It's almost always false and sounds arrogant.

8. **Never position competitors as the enemy.** The enemy is not speaking Dutch. Competitors are just different approaches to the same goal.
