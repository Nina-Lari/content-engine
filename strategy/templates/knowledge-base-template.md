# Master Knowledgebase Template

> This is your master source of truth. Every other strategy file derives from this document. Start here, be specific, and don't skip sections. See `strategy/examples/knowledge-base.md` for a complete example.

## Company Overview

### Vision
What future are you building toward? One sentence that describes the world you're trying to create.

[Example: "A world where every mid-market security team has enterprise-grade threat intelligence without the enterprise price tag."]

### Mission
What you do day-to-day to get there. Keep it under two sentences.

[Example: "We aggregate and contextualize threat data from 25,000+ global sources so security teams can make faster, more confident decisions."]

### Boilerplate
Your standard company description for press, bios, and partner pages. Write 2-3 sentences that someone unfamiliar with your company could read and immediately understand what you do, who you serve, and why it matters.

[Example: "[Company] is a threat intelligence platform built for mid-market security teams. We aggregate data from 25,000+ global sources to deliver contextual, actionable risk assessments — giving lean teams the same visibility that Fortune 500 security operations centers rely on. Founded in [year], [Company] serves [X] customers across [industries]."]

### What You Do (Plain Language)
Explain what your product does as if you're telling a smart friend who works in a different industry. No jargon. 3-5 sentences.

[Example: "We pull together safety and security data from thousands of sources — news, government reports, incident databases, social media — and turn it into a simple risk score for any location on Earth. Companies use it to decide where to send employees, where to open offices, and how to keep their people safe. Think of it as a weather forecast, but for threats."]

## Competitive Positioning

Map your competitive landscape at a high level. (For detailed competitor-by-competitor analysis, see the competitive landscape template.)

### Market Categories You Play In
List 1-3 market categories and your position in each.

- [Example: "Travel Risk Management — positioned as the data intelligence layer, not the full TRM platform."]
- [Example: "Threat Intelligence — positioned as the accessible alternative to enterprise-grade tools."]

### How You Win
In 2-3 sentences, summarize your core competitive advantage.

[Example: "We win on data breadth and speed-to-insight. Competitors either have broad data but poor UX, or good UX but narrow data. We're the first to combine 25,000+ sources with a sub-60-second assessment workflow."]

### Where You Lose (Honestly)

[Example: "We lose to [Competitor A] in accounts that need full travel management (booking, itinerary, comms) in one platform. We handle this by positioning as the intelligence layer that complements their existing TRM stack rather than replacing it."]
