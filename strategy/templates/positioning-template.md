# Positioning Template

> Derived from your master knowledgebase. Every differentiator needs a proof point — a specific number, case study, or customer outcome. If you can't prove it, it's not a differentiator, it's an aspiration. See `strategy/examples/positioning.md` for a complete example.

## Narrative Arc

Tell the story of your market in three acts. This is the strategic narrative that frames everything else — your content, your sales conversations, your investor pitch.

### Act 1: The Old World
How did things used to work? What was the status quo that everyone accepted?

[Example: "For twenty years, corporate security teams relied on a handful of government travel advisories and expensive consulting retainers to assess risk. The information was slow, generic, and designed for nation-state analysis — not for a security director deciding whether to send an engineering team to a client site in Bogota next Tuesday."]

### Act 2: The Disruption
What changed? What external forces broke the old model?

[Example: "Three things happened simultaneously: global travel volumes doubled, threat landscapes became hyperlocal (a city block can be high-risk while the neighborhood next to it is fine), and mid-market companies expanded internationally without scaling their security teams. The old tools — built for governments and Fortune 100 companies — couldn't serve this new reality. The result: thousands of companies making high-stakes decisions on outdated, incomplete information."]

### Act 3: The New Reality
What does the world look like with your solution in it? What's now possible that wasn't before?

[Example: "Today, a 3-person security team can generate a location risk assessment in under 60 seconds, drawing from 25,000+ sources updated in real-time. They can proactively alert travelers before they enter high-risk areas, brief executives with data instead of guesswork, and demonstrate duty-of-care compliance with an audit trail. The intelligence gap between a mid-market team and a Fortune 500 SOC has collapsed."]

## Positioning Statement (Summary)

Combine the above into a single positioning statement using this format:

**For** [target customer]
**who** [statement of need or opportunity],
**[Product]** is a [market category]
**that** [key benefit / reason to believe].
**Unlike** [primary competitive alternative],
**our product** [primary differentiation].

[Example:
"For mid-market security teams who need to assess global threats quickly and accurately, [Product] is a threat intelligence platform that delivers contextual risk assessments in under 60 seconds from 25,000+ sources. Unlike enterprise tools that require a dedicated analyst team, our product is designed for lean security teams that need enterprise-grade intelligence at mid-market scale."]
