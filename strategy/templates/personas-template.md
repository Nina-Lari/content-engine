# Personas Template

> Derived from your master knowledgebase. Write in the persona's voice, not your marketing voice — their pain points should sound like things they'd actually say in a meeting or Slack message, not how you'd describe them in a pitch deck. Repeat the template below for each persona (aim for 2-4). See `strategy/examples/personas.md` for a complete example.

## Persona 1: [Job Title]

### Role and Seniority

- **Title**: [Example: Head of Global Security]
- **Seniority level**: [Example: Director — reports to VP or C-suite]
- **Team size**: [Example: Manages 2-4 regional security analysts]
- **Scope of responsibility**: [What are they accountable for? Be specific.]
  - [Example: "Owns travel risk policy, executive protection program, and crisis response for all international operations. Responsible for 1,200+ travelers annually across 30+ countries."]

### Buying Psychology

Describe what drives this person's professional decisions. What motivates them day-to-day? What keeps them up at night?

- **Core motivation**: [Example: "Protecting people while enabling the business to operate globally. They see themselves as the person who makes international growth safe."]
- **Core fear**: [Example: "An incident that could have been prevented with better intelligence. Being the person who has to explain to leadership why they didn't see it coming."]
- **Definition of success**: [Example: "Zero preventable incidents. Board-level confidence in the security program. Being seen as a strategic enabler, not a cost center."]
- **Buying trigger**: [What pushes them from 'thinking about it' to 'actively evaluating'?]
  - [Example: "A near-miss incident, a new compliance mandate, or a leadership change that puts fresh eyes on their tooling."]

### Pain Points (In Their Words)

Write these as direct quotes — the way this person would describe their frustration to a peer, not how your sales team would summarize it.

1. [Example: "I spend every Monday morning pulling data from five different sources to build a weekly threat briefing that nobody reads."]
2. [Example: "When the CEO asks 'is it safe to send a team to Lagos next week?' I have to piece together an answer from three-day-old government advisories and Google News."]
3. [Example: "I know we need better tooling but every vendor demo I've sat through shows me a dashboard built for a 50-person SOC, not a 3-person team."]
4. [Example: "Our travel management system tells us WHERE people are going but nothing about what they're walking into."]
5. [Example: "..."]

### Decision Criteria

What does this persona evaluate when comparing solutions? Rank from most to least important.

1. [Example: **Data quality and freshness** — "If the data is 48 hours old, it's useless for travel risk decisions."]
2. [Example: **Ease of use** — "My analysts need to be productive in the first week, not the first quarter."]
3. [Example: **Integration with existing stack** — "If it doesn't connect to our travel management platform, it's a non-starter."]
4. [Example: **Credibility of risk methodology** — "I need to trust the scoring enough to stake my reputation on it."]
5. [Example: ...]

### Common Objections (In Their Words)

Write these as things this persona would actually say during a sales process. Include what's behind the objection.

| What They Say | What They Mean | How to Address It |
|---------------|---------------|-------------------|
| [Example: "We already have International SOS for this."] | [Example: They have a travel risk provider but it's slow and expensive — they're not getting proactive intelligence.] | [Example: Position as complementary intelligence layer, not a replacement for response services.] |
| [Example: "I don't have budget for this right now."] | [Example: They haven't built a business case to justify new tooling — need help framing the ROI.] | [Example: Provide ROI calculator showing hours saved per month on manual briefing work.] |
| [Example: "My team is too small to adopt another platform."] | [Example: Fear of implementation burden, not actual capacity issue.] | [Example: Show 15-minute onboarding flow and time-to-first-value stats from similar-sized teams.] |
| [Example: "..."] | [Example: ...] | [Example: ...] |

### Content Preferences

What formats, depth, and channels does this persona actually engage with?

- **Preferred formats**: [Example: Short-form insights (2-3 min reads), executive briefings, benchmark reports with peer comparisons]
- **Depth**: [Example: Strategic, not tactical. They want the "so what" and "what do I do about it" — not raw data.]
- **Channels**: [Example: LinkedIn (daily), industry email newsletters, ASIS conference content, direct peer recommendations]
- **Turn-offs**: [Example: "Anything that looks like a product pitch disguised as thought leadership. They've been burned by vendor content too many times."]

### Where They Congregate

List specific communities, events, publications, and online spaces where this persona spends time and is receptive to new ideas.

- **Professional communities**: [Example: ASIS International, OSAC (Overseas Security Advisory Council), Security Executive Council]
- **Events**: [Example: GSX (Global Security Exchange), Corporate Travel Risk Summit]
- **Publications**: [Example: Security Magazine, CSO Online, International SOS Risk Map (annual)]
- **Online**: [Example: LinkedIn groups for corporate security professionals, ASIS chapter Slack communities]
- **Podcasts/media**: [Example: The Security Podcasts, Travel Risk Management webinars]
