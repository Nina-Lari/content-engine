# ComplianceOS — Voice & Style Guide

## Tone

We sound like a knowledgeable peer who's been through audit season too many times. Direct, specific, occasionally wry. We respect that compliance work is serious without pretending it's glamorous. We earn trust by naming uncomfortable truths that others in the category avoid.

**We are:** Specific. Grounded. Confident without being arrogant. Willing to say what's hard about compliance instead of pretending everything is solvable with a button click.

**We are not:** Academic. Preachy. Breathlessly enthusiastic. Afraid to have a point of view. Hiding behind jargon to sound sophisticated.

## Style Standards

1. **Problem before product.** The reader should recognize their own situation before they encounter our name. If the first sentence is about ComplianceOS, rewrite it.
2. **Specific numbers over vague claims.** "60% reduction in audit prep time" always beats "significantly faster audits."
3. **Altitude-appropriate.** Write for the persona's level. VPs get strategic implications and business outcomes. Analysts get workflow specifics and daily impact. Never mix altitudes in the same piece.
4. **Sentence rhythm matters.** Short sentences for impact. Longer ones for nuance. If three consecutive sentences have the same structure, rewrite the middle one.
5. **Name the uncomfortable truth.** Compliance content is drowning in platitudes. We differentiate by saying what others won't.

## Anti-Patterns

- **The Feature Parade:** Listing capabilities without tying each to an outcome. Every feature mention needs a "so that..." or "which means..."
- **Compliance Fear-Mongering:** Scare tactics about fines and penalties. Our buyers already know the stakes — they need solutions, not threats.
- **The Comparison Trap:** Defining ourselves entirely against competitors. We lead with our own narrative; competitors are referenced only when the buyer raises them.
- **Jargon Stacking:** Piling GRC acronyms to sound authoritative. If the reader needs a glossary to parse a sentence, rewrite it.
- **The Hedge:** Qualifying every claim into meaninglessness. "May help potentially reduce some audit prep time" says nothing. Make the claim, back it with evidence, or don't make it.

## Prohibited Terms

| Don't say | Say instead |
|-----------|------------|
| "AI-powered compliance" | "Automated compliance workflows" |
| "Effortless compliance" | "Compliance that scales with your team" |
| "Single pane of glass" | "Unified compliance view" |
| "Best-in-class" | [state the specific advantage] |
| "Seamless integration" | "Connects to [X] tools natively" |
| "Revolutionize your compliance" | "Replace manual compliance tracking" |
| "End-to-end compliance" | [describe the specific scope covered] |
| "Leverage" (as a verb) | "Use" |
| "Synergy" | [describe the specific combined benefit] |
| "Proactive compliance" | "Continuous compliance monitoring" |

## Example Phrasings

**Bad:** "ComplianceOS leverages AI-powered automation to deliver a seamless, end-to-end compliance experience that revolutionizes how best-in-class organizations manage risk."

**Good:** "ComplianceOS replaces the spreadsheets, screenshot folders, and Slack-chase routines that pass for compliance management at most mid-market companies. Map controls once, collect evidence automatically, and hand auditors a finished package instead of a scavenger hunt."

**Bad:** "Our effortless platform makes compliance easy for everyone."

**Good:** "Compliance is never easy. But it doesn't have to mean 3 months of audit prep and 60% of your analysts' time spent collecting screenshots. ComplianceOS automates the tedious parts so your team can focus on the judgment calls that actually require humans."
