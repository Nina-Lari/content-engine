# ComplianceOS — Persona Profiles

## 1. VP of Compliance / Chief Compliance Officer

**Role context:** Owns the compliance program end-to-end. Reports to the CFO or General Counsel. Manages 2-8 analysts. Accountable for audit outcomes, regulatory risk posture, and increasingly, board-level compliance reporting.

**Buying psychology:** Risk-averse by training but frustrated enough with the status quo to consider change. Won't champion a tool that introduces migration risk. Needs to see that switching costs are lower than the compounding cost of staying. Evaluates vendor stability and implementation risk as heavily as features.

**Pain points (in their words):**
- "We spend 3 months prepping for every audit and still get findings we should have caught."
- "I can't give the board a real-time view of our compliance posture — everything is a manually assembled snapshot."
- "We're managing SOX in spreadsheets and SOC 2 in Vanta and there's zero overlap visibility."
- "I've added two analysts in the last year and we're still behind."

**Decision criteria:** Risk reduction proof, board-level reporting quality, multi-framework coverage depth, vendor financial stability, implementation timeline, total cost of ownership over 3 years.

**Common objections:** "We've already invested heavily in our current process." / "What's the implementation risk and rollback plan?" / "How do I justify this spend when we just hired more analysts?"

**Content preferences:** Executive-level strategic content. ROI frameworks, benchmark data, peer case studies. Does not read feature documentation — delegates that to their analysts. Responds to content that helps them build the internal business case.

**Where they congregate:** Compliance Week conferences, SCCE (Society of Corporate Compliance and Ethics), LinkedIn thought leadership, GRC-focused webinars, ISACA events. Active in private Slack communities for compliance leaders.

## 2. Head of Risk / Risk Management Director

**Role context:** Manages the enterprise risk register and risk assessment cadence. Sits between compliance and operations. First to recognize when compliance and risk management have become disconnected silos.

**Buying psychology:** Data-driven and analytically rigorous. Wants quantified risk reduction, not marketing claims. Skeptical of tools that promise automation without demonstrating how risk decisions actually improve. Values integration with existing risk frameworks and methodologies (COSO, NIST RMF).

**Pain points (in their words):**
- "Our risk register lives in a spreadsheet that's out of date the moment it's saved."
- "I can't connect risks to actual control effectiveness — it's all qualitative guesswork."
- "When a control fails, I find out during audit prep, not when it happens."
- "We treat risk assessment as an annual exercise, but our risk profile changes weekly."

**Decision criteria:** Risk quantification depth, real-time risk posture updates, dynamic scoring methodology, integration with existing ERM tools, audit trail integrity.

**Common objections:** "We need something that integrates with our existing ERM framework, not replaces it." / "How does this handle risks that span compliance and operational categories?" / "What's the data model behind your risk scoring?"

**Content preferences:** Analytical and methodology-focused. White papers on risk quantification, framework comparison guides, integration architecture documentation. Appreciates content that demonstrates intellectual rigor.

**Where they congregate:** RIMS (Risk and Insurance Management Society), FAIR Institute events, IRM (Institute of Risk Management), risk management LinkedIn groups. Reads Gartner and Forrester GRC reports.

## 3. IT Security Director / CISO

**Role context:** Owns technical controls and security infrastructure. Primary stakeholder for SOC 2 and ISO 27001. Often the person who bought Vanta or Drata initially and is now seeing its limitations as the company scaled past single-framework needs.

**Buying psychology:** Tool-savvy and integration-obsessed. Will evaluate API quality, integration depth, and whether the platform reduces their team's burden or creates another dashboard to maintain. Tests before trusting — will run a proof of concept on evidence automation accuracy before committing.

**Pain points (in their words):**
- "We bought Vanta for SOC 2 and it was great, but now we need SOX and HIPAA and it's a separate universe."
- "My team spends 20 hours a month taking screenshots and collecting evidence that should pull automatically."
- "I need continuous proof that controls are operating, not just point-in-time snapshots."
- "Every new framework means new manual processes layered on top of what we already can't maintain."

**Decision criteria:** Integration catalog breadth and depth, evidence automation accuracy rate, API quality and documentation, continuous monitoring granularity, SSO support and security posture of the tool itself.

**Common objections:** "We already have Vanta for SOC 2 — why would we switch everything?" / "How many of our tools do you integrate with natively vs. needing custom work?" / "What happens to our evidence history if we migrate?"

**Content preferences:** Technical and demo-focused. Integration guides, API documentation, architecture deep-dives, technical comparison matrices. Dismisses fluffy marketing content immediately. Responds to hands-on trials and sandbox environments.

**Where they congregate:** BSides conferences, RSA, Black Hat (more senior), InfoSec Slack/Discord communities, r/netsec, CISO-specific peer groups (Evanta, CISO Connect). Follows security influencers on Twitter/X and Mastodon.

## 4. GRC Analyst / Compliance Manager

**Role context:** The daily operator who collects evidence, tracks control status, coordinates with owners, and assembles audit packages. First person to feel the pain of manual processes. Most likely to champion a new tool internally. The person who built the 2,000-row compliance spreadsheet and now maintains it with a mix of dread and pride.

**Buying psychology:** Pragmatic and workflow-focused. Cares about day-one usability, not executive dashboards. Will advocate hard for tools that eliminate their most tedious tasks but will also kill an evaluation if the demo doesn't reflect their actual workflow. Needs to see their real daily work — chasing owners for evidence, mapping controls, pulling audit reports — reflected in the product.

**Pain points (in their words):**
- "I spend 60% of my week chasing control owners for evidence updates via email and Slack."
- "Our compliance spreadsheet has 2,000 rows and I'm terrified of breaking a formula."
- "When auditors ask for something, I'm scrambling across 5 different tools to compile it."
- "I know there's overlap between our SOC 2 and ISO 27001 controls but I can't prove it, so we test everything twice."

**Decision criteria:** Daily workflow automation, evidence collection burden reduction, notification and ownership tracking, import capability for existing data, reporting speed, learning curve.

**Common objections:** "Will this actually reduce my daily workload or just move it somewhere else?" / "How long does implementation take — we have an audit in 4 months." / "Can I import our existing 2,000-row control spreadsheet?"

**Content preferences:** Practical how-to content. Workflow guides, template libraries, implementation checklists, peer community forums. Values content they can use immediately over thought leadership. Watches product demo videos in full.

**Where they congregate:** GRC community forums (A-LIGN community, Tugboat Logic alumni), r/compliance, compliance-focused LinkedIn groups, ISACA local chapters, GRC-specific webinars. Active in product-specific user communities and Slack groups.
