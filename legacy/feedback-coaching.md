# Feedback and Coaching

> How Dutchwithjoost gives feedback. Use this document when writing about what happens when learners make mistakes, how they know they're improving, and why the app doesn't feel like a test.

---

## The Core Idea

Feedback changes based on where you are in a lesson:

| Phase | Goal | Feedback style |
|-------|------|----------------|
| Practice | Accurate production | Direct, specific, requires correction before moving on |
| Conversation | Fluent communication | Available but non-blocking, keeps conversation flowing |

**Why this matters for content:** This is a differentiator. Many apps either correct everything (feels like a test) or correct nothing (no improvement). Dutchwithjoost adapts feedback to the learning moment.

---

## Pronunciation Feedback (Read & Speak, Listen & Speak)

### What the learner experiences

1. Says the sentence aloud
2. Gets immediate result: pass or "words to improve"
3. If not good enough, sees which specific words need work
4. Can replay their own recording
5. Can replay just the difficult word from their recording
6. Can play the correct pronunciation for comparison
7. Sees pronunciation and completeness scores
8. Can adjust strictness level (more supportive or more demanding)

### Pain points this addresses

| Reader pain point | How to describe the feature |
|-------------------|----------------------------|
| "I don't know what I'm doing wrong" | "You see which specific words need work, not just 'try again'" |
| "I can't hear my own mistakes" | "You can replay your own recording and compare it to the correct pronunciation, word by word" |
| "The app says I'm wrong but doesn't help me fix it" | "For each flagged word, you hear how you said it and how it should sound" |
| "Pronunciation scoring feels arbitrary" | "You see scores for both pronunciation and completeness, and you can adjust how strict the scoring is" |

### Approved descriptions

- "You see exactly which words need work"
- "Compare your pronunciation to the correct one, word by word"
- "Replay your own recording to hear what the app heard"
- "Adjust the strictness to match your confidence level"

### Do not say

- "AI speech recognition" (describe what it does, not the technology)
- "Real-time feedback" (too vague)
- "Perfect pronunciation required" (not true — you can adjust strictness)

## Writing Feedback

### What the learner experiences

1. Sees the English meaning, writes the Dutch
2. If correct: simple "Good job!"
3. If incorrect: gets coaching, not just the answer
   - Feedback acknowledges what's already correct
   - Points to one thing to fix next
   - Gives hints without revealing the full answer
   - Shows example sentences demonstrating the pattern
4. If the Dutch is correct but misses target vocabulary: told "your Dutch is good, now use these words" with badges for the missing words
5. Answer reveal is delayed — app pushes for self-correction first

### Pain points this addresses

| Reader pain point | How to describe the feature |
|-------------------|----------------------------|
| "Apps just show me the answer when I'm wrong" | "Feedback guides you to discover the answer, not just reveals it" |
| "I feel discouraged when everything is marked wrong" | "Feedback tells you what's already correct, then focuses on one thing to fix" |
| "I know the Dutch is right but the app says it's wrong" | "If your Dutch is correct but uses different words, the app acknowledges that and asks you to try again with the target vocabulary" |
| "I learn more when I figure it out myself" | "The app delays showing the answer, giving you hints and examples to work it out first" |

### Approved descriptions

- "Feedback tells you what's already good, then guides you to fix one thing"
- "Hints help you discover the answer instead of just showing it"
- "If your Dutch is correct but misses the lesson vocabulary, you're not marked wrong — you're asked to try again with specific words"
- "The app coaches you toward the answer instead of judging your attempt"

### Do not say

- "Intelligent feedback" (too vague)
- "Corrects your grammar" (implies blocking correction; we guide, not block)

## Conversation Feedback (with Joost)

### What the learner experiences

1. Says something to Joost
2. Immediately sees whether the answer shows comprehension (answered the question vs. didn't answer)
3. Joost keeps responding — conversation continues
4. If there's a grammar issue, a feedback icon appears beside their message (doesn't interrupt)
5. Tapping the icon shows a short coaching note: the better phrasing, maybe a simple pattern to notice
6. After the conversation, can see overall feedback about patterns across multiple messages

### Pain points this addresses

| Reader pain point | How to describe the feature |
|-------------------|----------------------------|
| "I'm scared to make mistakes in conversation" | "Grammar feedback appears beside your message, not blocking the conversation. You can check it when you want to." |
| "Constant correction breaks my flow" | "Joost keeps responding even if your grammar isn't perfect. Corrections are available, not forced." |
| "I don't know if I'm actually communicating" | "You see immediately whether your answer made sense in the conversation, separate from whether the grammar was perfect" |
| "I make the same mistakes over and over" | "After the conversation, you get feedback on patterns — recurring habits worth noticing" |

### Approved descriptions

- "Grammar feedback appears beside your message, not blocking the conversation"
- "Joost keeps talking even if you make mistakes"
- "Corrections are available when you want them, not forced on every message"
- "You see whether you answered the question, not just whether your grammar was right"
- "After the conversation, you see patterns across your messages — the habits worth fixing"

### Do not say

- "Real-time grammar correction" (implies interruption)
- "AI tutor corrects you" (sounds like blocking correction)
- "Instant feedback" (too vague; be specific about what appears when)

## Progress and Completion Feedback

### What the learner experiences

- Always sees: current skill (read/listen/write), session number, progress bar, sentences remaining
- Chunks unlock in order — locked state shows what's next
- At end of session: completion summary with spoken-correctly count, written-correctly count, new words introduced, next action
- XP animations on correct actions

### Pain points this addresses

| Reader pain point | How to describe the feature |
|-------------------|----------------------------|
| "I don't know how much is left" | "You always see how many sentences are left in the session" |
| "I don't know what to do next" | "The interface shows what's active, what's done, and what's next" |
| "I finished but don't feel like I accomplished anything" | "Each session ends with a summary: what you practiced, what you got right, what's next" |

### Approved descriptions

- "You always know where you are and how much is left"
- "Each session ends with a clear summary of what you practiced"
- "The course guides you step by step — you don't have to figure out what to study next"

## Content Angles by Reader Concern

Use this when matching article topics to feedback features:

| Article about... | Lead with... |
|------------------|--------------|
| "Will this app help my pronunciation?" | Word-level feedback, playback comparison, adjustable strictness |
| "I'm scared to speak Dutch" | Conversation feedback is non-blocking, Joost keeps responding, corrections are optional |
| "Apps just tell me I'm wrong" | Writing feedback coaches toward answer, acknowledges what's correct first |
| "I want to practice speaking without judgment" | Comprehension check (did you communicate?) separate from grammar check |
| "I make the same mistakes repeatedly" | Pattern feedback after conversations |
| "I don't know if I'm making progress" | Progress visibility, session summaries, clear next steps |
